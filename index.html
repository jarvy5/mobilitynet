Project Overview

Open-Source Cyberinfrastructure for Cross-Domain Mobility Data

Human mobility data from urban infrastructure has enormous scientific potential — but most of it is proprietary, costly, or privacy-sensitive. MobilityNet builds the tools to generate synthetic datasets that are open, application-ready, and privacy-guaranteed.

Three CI Components
1

Cross-Domain Real Data Curation

Addresses data bias by integrating mobility signals across domains — cellular networks, transit systems, financial transactions, and urban sensors — into a unified, validated corpus.

2

Socially-Informed Real Data Interpretation

Uses large language models to address data implicitness — enriching sparse or ambiguous mobility records with semantic context about purpose, behavioral patterns, and urban dynamics.

3

Privacy-Preserving Synthetic Data Generation

Generates synthetic mobility datasets using validated generative ML (diffusion models) and differential privacy techniques, balancing utility, privacy, and demographic fairness.

Research Applications

Transportation & Urban Planning

Analytical and predictive models for infrastructure design, congestion, and autonomous driving systems.

Public Health & Epidemiology

Mobility-based pandemic mitigation modeling and emergency response optimization.

5G Network Deployment

Demand-driven antenna placement and network capacity planning using synthetic population flows.

Economics & Behavioral Research

Understanding consumer behavior, economic activity patterns, and spatial inequality through movement data.

Geography & Urban Science

Cross-city spatial analysis, neighborhood characterization, and point-of-interest dynamics.

Privacy & Fairness Research

Benchmarking differential privacy mechanisms and auditing demographic representativeness in datasets.

Tools & Software

Open-source tools developed by the MobilityNet team for synthetic mobility data generation and privacy analysis.

GeoGen Trajectory Generation New
View on GitHub
A two-stage coarse-to-fine framework for fine-grained synthetic location-based social network trajectory generation. Generates realistic, privacy-safe human mobility trajectories at scale. Published at AAAI 2026.
👤 Rongchao Xu et al. 📄 AAAI 2026 🔗 github.com/Rongchao98/GeoGen
AutoSTDiff Trajectory Generation
View on GitHub
Autoregressive spatio-temporal denoising diffusion model for asynchronous trajectory generation. Generates high-fidelity synthetic mobility trajectories using a diffusion-based approach with strong temporal consistency. Published at SDM 2025.
👤 Rongchao Xu et al. 📄 SDM 2025 🔗 github.com/Rongchao98/AutoSTDiff
POIPrivacy Privacy Analysis
View on GitHub
Demo codebase for studying privacy risk in point-of-interest recommendation systems. Includes core implementations of attack functions to audit and benchmark privacy vulnerabilities in location-based services. Published at KDD 2024.
👤 Kunlin Choi et al. 📄 KDD 2024 🔗 github.com/KunlinChoi/POIPrivacy
